# 🤖 AI Prompt Frameworks for Tech Recruitment (for Gemini) ("Gems")

Over the past 1–2 years, I’ve saved a huge amount of time using AI in tech recruitment.

But I’ve also wasted time constantly rewriting prompts and tweaking outputs that weren’t consistent.

What I needed was something more structured — prompts I could reuse that would consistently deliver the right outputs. 🔁

So I spent time refining them properly.

The result? A set of structured, reusable prompt frameworks designed to support the core parts of my workflow — from messy notes to polished outputs. 🧩

These frameworks help with:

- 📝 Turning rough intake notes into clear, structured briefs  
- 📩 Improving outreach messaging  
- 🎯 Screening candidates more objectively  
- 🗂 Structuring interview feedback  
- 🧠 Mapping talent markets  
- 📊 Creating more consistent, high-quality shortlists  

They’re not perfect (and still evolving). ⚙️  

I’ll keep updating them in this repo.

They’ve definitely reduced admin time, improved consistency across the team, and freed me up to focus on what actually matters — conversations, relationships, and hiring quality. 🤝

## 🔄 The Workflow of Gems

Below is a visual overview of how these Gems connect into a structured, end-to-end recruitment workflow.

Rather than standalone prompts, they’re designed as a **linked system** that supports the full hiring lifecycle — from intake to decision.

![Workflow of Gems](./workflow-of-gems.png)

It follows five clear stages:

### 🔵 1. Define the Role
Turn messy intake notes into clear briefs, structured job descriptions, aligned hiring criteria, and strong job posts.

### 🔴 2. Map the Market
Understand talent availability, competitor landscapes, and market dynamics before outreach even begins.

### 🟡 3. Engage Talent
Generate tailored, high-quality outreach messaging designed to improve response rates and candidate experience.

### 🟢 4. Assess & Screen
Introduce more objectivity into CV reviews and screening notes using consistent evaluation frameworks.

### 🔵 5. Make the Decision
Structure interview feedback, reduce bias, and support clearer, evidence-based hiring decisions — right through to onboarding.
